São Paulo, Brazil

I live in the state of São Paulo, Brazil. With 46 million inhabitants—accounting for about 22% of Brazil's population—it is the most populous state in Brazil and the most populous national subdivision in the Americas, boasting more inhabitants than any South American country other than Argentina and Colombia. The population of São Paulo is among the most diverse in the country, descending primarily from Italians (who began emigrating there in the late 19th century), Portuguese (who colonized Brazil and established the region's first European settlements), indigenous Amerindians, Africans, and migrants from other parts of Brazil. Other major waves of immigration—including Arabs, Germans, Chinese, Spaniards, and Japanese—have also played a significant role in the ethnic makeup of the local population.
